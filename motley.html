Motley Editorial Layout Design

Motley is an photography brand that markets towards the Millenial and Generation Z Audience. The process of designing this layout began with brainstorming the typefaces and images that would match the atmosphere of the brand. The title "Motley" routes to the direct definition of the word that means exhibiting great diversity of elements or being of different colors combined. The style of the brand is based off artistic pop culture and uses vibrant colours. The greatest difficulty making this layout was working with the type and images to feel combined and maintaining the readability of the content. To finalize the design, the typeface was repetitively used throughout the layout to keep consistency and maintain a sense of harmony.

Lighting Techniques

Colour Gels

The first layout of the magazine starts with a article on colour gels and lighting techniques based on the colour gels. The type is designed and placed methodically around the image to maintain readability and sense of unity with the image.

Framing Techniques

Getting the Angle

This article covers framing techniques, focusing mainly on angles and thirds. The design choice behind this layout was to emphasize the angle of the model. Therefore to prevent locking the type into a triangle, columns were used to maintain the angle and prevent difficulty of readability.

Settings Techniques

The Perfect Night Shot

"The Perfect Night Shot" layout focuses on camera settings techniques in the dark. The article was designed to help unify the sense of parallelism throughout the photograph. The simplicity of the layout allows the eyes to capture the photography and read through the article smoothly

Portrait Techniques

Painting with Colour

The term of painting with light is taken to another level for portaiture. This article covers a new perspective to creative lighting in portrait photography. The design of the layout is more open and allows the reader to follow along the article while simultaneously taking in the photograph.
